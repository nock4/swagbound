silence detection, and frame sampling on 2026-07-07. Timestamps are from the video
(player-paced, so treat as upper bounds on a relaxed first playthrough).

## The five pacing laws extracted

1. THE SLOW ON-RAMP: ~3.5 minutes elapse before the game world appears at all
   (logos -> slow title -> naming). Anticipation is built with almost no content.
2. IDENTITY BEFORE WORLD: the naming ritual (~70s of player-paced input) makes the
   player commit before the first scene. It is the single cheapest engagement tool
   in the game.
3. QUIET IS THE DEFAULT: the opening night runs on rumble, room tone, and sparse
   cues. Music ENTERING is an event; music STOPPING marks the two most important
   story moments (meteor site, prophecy). Wall-to-wall music erases this tool.
4. COMBAT IS EARNED: first battle at ~9 minutes. The world is safe until the story
   sends you somewhere unsafe. Enemies-on-screen-from-second-one breaks this.
5. ONE LOOP, ONE LESSON: 20 minutes = exactly one out-and-back (house -> hill ->
   meteor -> house) with one wild battle and one story battle. Story progression is
   a single clean arc, not a chain of gates.
