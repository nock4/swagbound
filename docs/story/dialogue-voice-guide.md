# Swagbound Dialogue Voice Guide

The one rule: **EarthBound delivery, Swagbound vocabulary.**

A plain, kid-real narrator/cast (Itoi voice) carrying our own words (Milady, Drifella,
Little Swag World). The verbatim corpus quotes stay raw; the connective tissue around
them goes plain and casual. The friction between those two registers IS the tone.

---

## 1. The voice (how things are said)

- **Plain and casual.** Characters talk like real kids and tired adults, even when the
  world is ending. No thespian language, no prophecy-oracle cadence, no "hark."
- **Mundane meets bizarre.** Frame cosmic horror with flat, domestic calm. Weapons and
  threats are described matter-of-factly ("It copies people. It's honestly super rude
  about it.").
- **Casual about horror.** The scary thing gets understated, not underlined. Let the
  reader feel the drop; don't announce it.
- **Sincere underneath.** The jokes are a veneer over a real emotional beat. Land the
  feeling plainly, once, then move on. Don't over-explain it.
- **Occasionally self-aware.** Sparing fourth-wall winks (saving, this being a game) are
  in-bounds but rare. A seasoning, not the meal.
- **Ellipses and short lines.** "...Oh. Hey." Dream/telepathy especially leans on trailing
  dots and pauses.

### Cloak reference (the calibrated target)
> "...Oh. Hey. You can hear me? Huh. That usually doesn't work."
> "So, there's a thing. It copies people. It's honestly super rude about it."
> "When you wake up, someone's gonna hand you something. Don't lose it. It's the only
> thing that thing can't fake."

---

## 2. The vocabulary (what is said) - keep ours

- **Milady:** "milady," twin, correction / corrected / filing / the file, the version that
  got out. The antagonist machinery's record-speak.
- **Drifella / Little Swag World:** existing lore, names, and texture stay.
- Do not sand our nouns down into generic JRPG words. EarthBound the *grammar*, not the
  proper nouns.

---

## 3. Pull-quotes = found-speech (KEEP VERBATIM)

Direct lines mined from the Twitter sessions and the lore corpus are **found-speech**.
They are the uncanny texture. Rules:

- **Do not rewrite them into the plain voice.** They stay raw, odd, uninflected.
- Frame them: a plain kid-voiced character sets up, the corpus quote drops, a plain
  character reacts. Normal -> eerie verbatim -> normal. That whiplash is the point.
- A corrected NPC often speaks ONLY in corpus-quote (they sound like the broadcast). An
  uncorrected one sounds like a person reacting to it.

---

## 4. Show-don't-tell still governs (unchanged)

Key story moments and guidance are ACTIONS and scenes, never monologue/narration. The
only narration allowed is a light wayfinding hint ("...I should go check out the arcade.").
This guide sets the *voice* of the speech we do write; it does not license exposition
dumps. (See act1-direction / the corrections direction.)

## 5. No em dashes in player-facing text.

Zero. Grep every addition. Use periods, commas, or ellipses.
